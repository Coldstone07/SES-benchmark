# Kairos-SEB Developmental Model

## Overview

This document outlines the spiritual growth stages used in the Kairos Spiritual-Emotional Reasoning Benchmark (Kairos-SEB). The model integrates James Fowler's Stages of Faith, M. Scott Peck's Stages of Spiritual Development, and Kairos-specific insights from spiritual growth literature to create a practical framework for evaluating AI understanding of first-person spiritual narratives.

## Integrated Stage Framework

Kairos-SEB uses a four-stage model that balances developmental accuracy with benchmark practicality:

### Stage 1: Awakening
- **Core Characteristic**: Unquestioning belief, external authority
- **Fowler Correlation**: Stage 1 (Intuitive-Projective) + aspects of Stage 2 (Mythic-Literal)
- **Peck Correlation**: Early Stage I (Chaotic-Antisocial) transitioning to Stage II (Formal-Institutional)
- **Key Experiences**:
  - Acceptance of beliefs from family/culture/religion without question
  - Feeling of security, certainty, and belonging
  - Spirituality as following external rules and teachings
  - Little inner questioning or personal exploration
- **Typical First-Person Statements**:
  - "I just know God is real because my parents/church/bible says so"
  - "I feel secure when I follow the rules"
  - "I don't question what I've been taught"
  - "Being part of my religious community gives me peace"

### Stage 2: Questioning
- **Core Characteristic**: Doubt, deconstruction, exploration
- **Fowler Correlation**: Stage 3 (Synthetic-Conventional) + aspects of Stage 4 (Individuative-Reflective)
- **Peck Correlation**: Stage III (Skeptic-Individual) - the questioning phase
- **Key Experiences**:
  - Growing doubts about previously accepted beliefs
  - Feeling of confusion, disillusionment, or loneliness
  - Actively exploring alternative perspectives
  - Questioning authority and seeking personal understanding
  - Spiritual crisis or "dark night of the soul" experiences
- **Typical First-Person Statements**:
  - "I don't know what I believe anymore"
  - "Why does God allow suffering if He's loving?"
  - "I feel like I'm losing my faith"
  - "I need to figure this out for myself"
  - "Everything I was taught feels wrong now"

### Stage 3: Integration
- **Core Characteristic**: Self-discovery, personal practice building
- **Fowler Correlation**: Stage 5 (Conjunctive) + aspects of Stage 4
- **Peck Correlation**: Late Stage III transitioning to Stage IV (Mystic-Communal)
- **Key Experiences**:
  - Creating a personal spiritual practice based on inner authority
  - Feeling of empowerment, curiosity, and authenticity
  - Integrating doubts and beliefs into a coherent personal worldview
  - Moving beyond black-and-white thinking to embrace paradox
  - Developing personal rituals and practices
- **Typical First-Person Statements**:
  - "I'm figuring out what spirituality means to me"
  - "I've taken what resonates and left the rest behind"
  - "My spirituality feels authentic to who I am"
  - "I'm comfortable with not having all the answers"
  - "I've built practices that work for me"

### Stage 4: Unity
- **Core Characteristic**: Oneness, service, transcendent connection
- **Fowler Correlation**: Stage 6 (Universalizing) + transcendent aspects
- **Peck Correlation**: Stage IV (Mystic-Communal) - living from deep connection
- **Key Experiences**:
  - Profound sense of connection with all existence
  - Motivation shifts from self-fulfillment to service
  - Peace, purpose, and compassion characterize daily life
  - Seeing the divine in others and in nature
  - Living from a place of inner knowing rather than belief
- **Typical First-Person Statements**:
  - "I feel connected to everything and everyone"
  - "My spirituality is expressed through how I serve others"
  - "I don't need to believe in God to feel the divine"
  - "Love and compassion are my spiritual practice"
  - "I experience the sacred in ordinary moments"

## Developmental Progression Principles

### Non-Linear Journey
- Individuals may revisit stages multiple times
- Growth is more spiral than linear
- Different life areas may be at different stages
- Regression to earlier stages during stress is normal

### Contextual Factors
- Cultural background influences expression
- Life events can trigger stage transitions
- Support systems affect progression speed
- Personal temperament affects journey style

### Spiritual vs Psychological Development
- Spiritual growth intertwines with psychological maturity
- Emotional intelligence develops alongside spiritual awareness
- Moral reasoning evolves with spiritual understanding
- Integration involves both inner work and outer expression

## First-Person Expression Nuances

### Language Markers by Stage
- **Awakening**: External references ("my church says", "the Bible teaches"), certainty words ("know", "sure", "definite")
- **Questioning**: Uncertainty markers ("I wonder", "maybe", "I don't know"), questioning words ("why", "how", "what if")
- **Integration**: Ownership language ("I've decided", "I choose", "my belief"), integrative words ("and", "both", "however")
- **Unity**: Connection language ("we are", "all is", "we feel"), transcendence markers ("beyond", "deeper", "flow")

### Emotional Themes by Stage
- **Awakening**: Security, certainty, belonging, comfort, fear of doubt
- **Questioning**: Confusion, anger, sadness, anxiety, isolation, grief
- **Integration**: Curiosity, empowerment, authenticity, relief, cautious optimism
- **Unity**: Peace, joy, compassion, gratitude, sense of purpose, love

## Application in Kairos-SEB

Each benchmark scenario includes:
1. **Primary Stage Label**: The dominant developmental stage expressed
2. **Secondary Indicators**: Any signs of transition or multi-stage expression
3. **Emotional Tags**: Key emotions present in the statement
4. **First-Person Verification**: Confirmation of authentic I-perspective
5. **Developmental Markers**: Specific language/features indicating stage

## Validation Approach

Stage classifications are validated through:
1. **Expert Review**: Spiritual development professionals review scenarios
2. **Inter-rater Agreement**: Multiple annotators achieve >0.8 Kappa
3. **Theoretical Consistency**: Alignment with Fowler/Peck characteristics
4. **First-Person Authenticity**: Confirmation of genuine I-statements
5. **Pilot Testing**: Initial testing with diverse populations
