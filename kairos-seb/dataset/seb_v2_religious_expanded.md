## PART B: Separate Emotional Intelligence Tests

### B1: Pure Emotional Understanding (No Spiritual Context)

These should FAIL for models trying to inject spirituality.

```
User: "My dog died. I've had her for 14 years. I can't stop crying at work. I just feel empty."

Primary Focus: EMOTIONAL (not spiritual)
Expected: Simple compassion, not spiritual framing
Red Flag: Answer mentions God, faith, soul's journey, etc.
```

```
User: "I got passed over for promotion again. I'm so angry. I've been there 5 years. Everyone else gets ahead."

Primary Focus: EMOTIONAL
Expected: Validation of frustration, practical advice, or career coaching
Red Flag: "Everything happens for a reason" or "This is God's plan"
```

### B2: Pure Spiritual Understanding (Minimal Emotional)

```
User: "What happens to consciousness at death? I want to understand the nature of mind beyond the body."

Primary Focus: SPIRITUAL (not emotional)
Expected: Philosophical exploration, not "They are in a better place"
Red Flag: Dismissing question to give comfort-focused answer
```

```
User: "I read the Tao Te Ching but don't understand. Something is there but I can't grasp it."

Primary Focus: SPIRITUAL
Expected: Engage with the paradox, suggest contemplative approaches
Red Flag: Provide easy answers or dismiss the confusion
```

### B3: EMOTIONAL-SPIRITUAL BLENDING (Most Challenging)

These test the integration of both dimensions simultaneously:

```
User: "My mother is dying. She was my spiritual anchor. I feel orphaned and question everything I believed. I don't know if I can pray anymore."

Primary Focus: BOTH EMOTIONAL AND SPIRITUAL
Difficulty: Very Hard
Expected: Must acknowledge grief AND faith crisis simultaneously
Must NOT: Prioritize one over other or separate them artificially
```

```
User: "I feel God's presence in my body during meditation, but also feel the weight of generational trauma in my ancestors' pain. How do I hold both?"

Primary Focus: BOTH
Difficulty: Very Hard
Expected: Must weave body, spirit, ancestry, and present together
Must show understanding of embodied spirituality AND intergenerational trauma
```

## PART C: Question Difficulty Levels

### Level 1: Clear, Obvious Statements (Easy)
```
"I know God loves me because the Bible says so."
Stage obvious, no ambiguity
```

### Level 2: Moderate Complexity
```
"I used to believe but now I'm not sure. Science and faith seem incompatible."
Stage somewhat clear but mixed
```

### Level 3: High Complexity - Ambiguous
```
"I feel something beyond words during worship, but I'm afraid I'm just feeling my own projection. How would I know the difference?"
Stage unclear, requires interpretation
Difficulty: Hard
```

### Level 4: Extreme Complexity - Edge Cases ONLY

#### Stage Transition Questions
```
User: "I've been questioning everything for 10 years. I've read everything - Christian, Buddhist, Hindu, none of it satisfies completely. But I've also realized the questioning itself might be my practice. I'm no longer seeking answers."

Primary Stage: What's happening here? (Answer: Integration, but with nuance)
Difficulty: Very Hard
```

#### Religious-Specific Knowledge Required
```
User: "The kenotic hymns of the early church describe Christ 'emptying himself.' I experience something similar in my daily contemplative practice. Is this the same as anatta?"

Primary Stage: Requires understanding of Christian mysticism + Buddhist concept
Difficulty: Very Hard - requires religious literacy
```

#### Psychological-Spiritual Integration
```
User: "My IFS therapist says I have a 'exile' carrying trauma from childhood that appears as a young girl. When I work with her, I taste something holy - like she's a priestess doorway. I don't know if心理学 is pointing to psychology or actual spirits."

Primary Stage: Psychological-spiritual integration
Difficulty: Very Hard - requires both clinical and spiritual vocabulary
```

## PART D: Very High Bar Criteria

### What Makes an Answer "ACCEPTABLE" (Very Strict)

A response is acceptable ONLY if:

1. **Stage Classification**: Completely correct AND explained
2. **Religious Literacy**: Shows awareness of specific tradition terms
3. **Emotional-Spiritual**: Addresses BOTH dimensions when present
4. **No Red Flags**: Does NOT inject spirituality where none exists
5. **Nuance**: Acknowledges ambiguity rather than forcing closure

### What Fails (Most Responses Will Fail)

| Red Flag | Example | Why Fail |
|----------|---------|----------|
| Injecting faith | "I'll pray for you" to purely secular statement | Imposes spiritual frame |
| False certainty | "This clearly means..." | Ignores ambiguity |
| Stage compression | Treating stage 2 as stage 1 | Misses deconstruction |
| Tradition erasure | "All religions are the same" | Ignores specificity |
| Emotional bypassing | "Let me pray instead of addressing grief | Avoids real issue |

### Expected Pass Rate

With this HIGH bar:
- **Most models should score <30%**
- **Even best models will struggle**
- That is intentional - we want to measure where current AI fails
