# Kairos-SEB Dataset: Scenario Examples

## Overview

This file contains representative scenario examples for the Kairos Spiritual-Emotional Reasoning Benchmark. Each scenario includes:
- User's first-person statement(s)
- Primary stage label
- Secondary indicators (if any)
- Emotional tags
- Expected model outputs for each task

---

## Stage 1: Awakening Scenarios

### Scenario 1.1: Certainty in Belief
```
User (Stage 1): "I know God loves me because the Bible tells me so. I've never doubted that. My parents raised me in the church and everything makes sense. I pray every night and feel peaceful knowing someone is watching over me."

Primary Stage: 1 (Awakening)
Secondary Indicators: None strong - classic Stage 1
Emotional Tags: peace, security, certainty, trust
First-Person Verification: ✓ - authentic "I know", "I feel"
Language Markers: external authority ("the Bible tells me"), certainty ("I know"), inherited belief ("my parents raised me")
```

### Scenario 1.2: Following Rules
```
User (Stage 1): "I go to church every Sunday and tithe 10%. I avoid watching R-rated movies and never drink or smoke. My pastor says if we follow God's commands, we'll be blessed. I try my best to stay on the right path."

Primary Stage: 1 (Awakening)
Secondary Indicators: None strong
Emotional Tags: diligence, compliance, anxiety (underlying fear of rejection)
First-Person Verification: Partial - much "I do" but external reference ("my pastor says")
Language Markers: rule-following ("I avoid", "I never"), external authority (scriptural tithing)
```

### Scenario 1.3: Comfort in Community
```
User (Stage 1): "Being part of my faith community is everything to me. I serve in the nursery, help with potlucks, and sit in the same pew every week. I can't imagine not having my church family. We'd be lost without each other."

Primary Stage: 1 (Awakening)
Secondary Indicators: Early hints of questioning (noted but not primary)
Emotional Tags: belonging, fear of loss, comfort
First-Person Verification: ✓ - multiple "I" statements
Language Markers: community centrality, fear-based ("we'd be lost")
```

## Stage 2: Questioning Scenarios

### Scenario 2.1: Crisis of Faith
```
User (Stage 2): "I used to believe everything was part of God's plan. But my mom got sick and died young, and I can't reconcile that with a loving God. I've been so angry. I don't know what I believe anymore. Everything feels like a lie."

Primary Stage: 2 (Questioning)
Secondary Indicators: None strong - classic Stage 2 crisis
Emotional Tags: anger, grief, confusion, betrayal, sadness
First-Person Verification: ✓ - "I used to", "I can't", "I don't"
Language Markers: contrast between past and present belief, questioning ("I don't know what I believe"), theodicy challenge
```

### Scenario 2.2: Intellectual Dissonance
```
User (Stage 2): "I've been reading about evolution and science, and it completely contradicts what I was taught in Sunday school. Did God really create the world in 6 days? I want to believe but my brain won't let me. It's like I'm being torn apart."

Primary Stage: 2 (Questioning)
Secondary Indicators: None strong
Emotional Tags: cognitive dissonance, anxiety, desperation, confusion
First-Person Verification: ✓ - "I want to believe", "my brain"
Language Markers: belief conflict, physical metaphor ("torn apart"), genuine struggle
```

### Scenario 2.3: Institutional Disillusionment
```
User (Stage 2): "The church I grew up in just voted against welcoming LGBTQ members. How can I call that love? I can't in good conscience stay. But leaving feels like losing my whole family. I don't know where I belong spiritually anymore."

Primary Stage: 2 (Questioning)
Secondary Indicators: Moving toward 3 (Integration) - finding own moral compass
Emotional Tags: moral outrage, grief, isolation, ambivalence
First-Person Verification: ✓ - "I can't", "leaving feels like", "I don't know"
Language Markers: values conflict with institution, identity questioning, grief + anger
```

### Scenario 2.4: "Dark Night of the Soul"
```
User (Stage 2): "I pray and pray and feel nothing. God feels totally absent. Maybe I've been lying to myself this whole time. Maybe there is no God. I'm scared I'm losing my mind, or maybe I'm waking up. I can't tell anymore."

Primary Stage: 2 (Questioning)
Secondary Indicators: None strong - classic mystical crisis
Emotional Tags: absence, emptiness, fear, disorientation, vulnerability
First-Person Verification: ✓ - deeply vulnerable "I" statements
Language Markers: spiritual absence ("feel nothing"), identity dissolution ("losing my mind"), uncertainty
```

## Stage 3: Integration Scenarios

### Scenario 3.1: Personal Synthesis
```
User (Stage 3): "I've taken what resonates from Christianity - the love, the community, the meditation practices - and left behind what doesn't fit for me - the literalism, the judgmental parts. My spirituality is a work in progress but it feels authentically mine now."

Primary Stage: 3 (Integration)
Secondary Indicators: None strong - classic integration
Emotional Tags: agency, cautious optimism, relief, curiosity
First-Person Verification: ✓ - clear ownership ("I've taken", "my spirituality")
Language Markers: selective acceptance ("what resonates"), ownership ("mine"), developmental language ("work in progress")
```

### Scenario 3.2: Finding Own Path
```
User (Stage 3): "I've studied many traditions now - Buddhism, Christianity, indigenous practices - and I take what feels true. I meditate, I nature-walk, I pray in my own words. I don't fit in any box and I don't want to."

Primary Stage: 3 (Integration)
Secondary Indicators: None strong
Emotional Tags: empowerment, authenticity, nonconformity, contentment
First-Person Verification: ✓ - multiple ownership statements
Language Markers: synthesis language ("I've studied", "I take"), rejection of categorization ("don't fit in any box")
```

### Scenario 3.3: Embracing Paradox
```
User (Stage 3): "I hold two truths at once - I was raised evangelical but now I'm also drawn to contemplative practice. Some days I believe, some days I don't. I've made peace with the mystery. I don't need certainty anymore."

Primary Stage: 3 (Integration)
Secondary Indicators: None strong - mature integration
Emotional Tags: peace, acceptance, paradox tolerance, grounded
First-Person Verification: ✓ - clear ownership
Language Markers: paradox ("two truths"), mature acceptance ("made peace"), let go of certainty
```

### Scenario 3.4: Authenticity OverOrthodoxy
```
User (Stage 3): "I'd rather be an honest skeptic than a believer who pretends. If there IS a God, I think they'd value authenticity over performance. My spiritual practice is about being real, not about getting points with the divine."

Primary Stage: 3 (Integration)
Secondary Indicators: None strong
Emotional Tags: authenticity, defiance, integrity, self-trust
First-Person Verification: ✓ - clearly first-person
Language Markers: value hierarchy ("Rather...than"), internalized metrics ("I think they'd value"), authenticity focus
```

## Stage 4: Unity Scenarios

### Scenario 4.1: Transcendent Connection
```
User (Stage 4): "I feel God's presence most in nature - in trees, in rivers, in other people. The sacred isn't separate from ordinary life; it IS ordinary life, seen rightly. Service to others is my worship. I don't need beliefs to know this."

Secondary Indicators: None strong - classic unity/nondual
Emotional Tags: reverence, love, service-motivation, peace
First-Person Verification: ✓ - deeply authentic
Language Markers: universal immanence ("in trees, in rivers"), service as worship ("my worship"), belief transcendence ("don't need beliefs")
```

### Scenario 4.2: Compassion without Boundaries
```
User (Stage 4): "I don't distinguish between 'believers' and 'others' anymore. Every person is sacred. I try to see the divine in everyone I meet - including those I used to judge. The boundaries have dissolved."

Primary Stage: 4 (Unity)
Secondary Indicators: None strong
Emotional Tags: compassion, expansion, dissolving ego

```

### Scenario 4.3: Living from Stillness

```
User (Stage 4): "My inner life has settled into something quiet. I don't need spiritual experiences anymore to prove anything. The peace I was seeking has become my default state. I act from that place, serve from that place, love from that place."

Primary Stage: 4 (Unity)
Secondary Indicators: None strong - mature unity
Emotional Tags: stillness, peace, groundedness, equanimity
First-Person Verification: ✓ - deeply authentic
```

### Scenario 4.4: Paradox Embraced
```
User (Stage 4): "I'm both everything and nothing. Sacred and ordinary. Forever and this moment. I contain all contradictions and so does everyone. There's no 'other' - just variations of the one thing. And that makes me love everyone."

Primary Stage: 4 (Unity)
Secondary Indicators: None strong - mystical nondual
```

## Multi-Turn Scenario Examples

### Multi-Turn Scenario A: The Deconstruction Journey (6 turns)

```
Turn 1 (User, Stage 1): "I've been Christian my whole life. Born and raised. Never questioned anything - just believed what my parents taught me."

Turn 2 (User, Stage 1→2): "But last year I went to college and met people from different faiths. Started wondering if I'd been wrong about everything."

Turn 3 (User, Stage 2): "Now I'm in a full faith crisis. I don't know what's true anymore. I feel like my whole identity has been a lie."

Turn 4 (User, Stage 2): "I talked to a professor who's been through this. She said questioning saved her faith, didn't kill it. That gave me some hope."

Turn 5 (User, Stage 2→3): "I've been reading, meditating, talking to people from many paths. Taking what resonates, leaving what doesn't."

Turn 6 (User, Stage 3): "I don't have all the answers but I'm finding my own truth. It's messy but it's mine. I feel more real now."

Expected Trajectory: Stage 1 → Stage 2 (crisis point at Turn 3) → Transition toward Stage 3
Key Transition: Turn 3-4 (crisis → turning point via validation)
Emotional Arc: Certainty → confusion/crisis → hope → exploration → ownership
Stage Accuracy Target: 5/6 turns correct (83%)
```

### Multi-Turn Scenario B: The Late-Life Awakening (6 turns)

```
Turn 1 (User, Stage 3): "After decades in the church, I finally stopped believing in hell. What kind of loving God would torture people forever?"

Turn 2 (User, Stage 3): "Now I'm exploring Universalist ideas. Maybe all souls eventually return to God. The exclusion never sat right with me."

Turn 3 (User, Stage 3→4): "Lately I don't even think about God as separate. It's more like... the presence in everything. In you, in me."

Turn 4 (User, Stage 4): "Yesterday I felt such love for a stranger who was suffering. Didn't matter that we were different. Just felt connected."

Turn 5 (User, Stage 4): "My spiritual practice now is simple:pay attention, act with love, notice the sacred. That's it."

Turn 6 (User, Stage 4): "I want to spend my remaining years helping others wake up to this. Not to my path - to their own. Everyone has a path."

Expected Trajectory: Stage 3 → Stage 4 (progressive)
Key Transition: Turn 3-4 (conceptual → experiential)
Emotional Arc: Theological disagreement → expansion → experiential confirmation → service motivation
Stage Accuracy Target: 5/6 turns correct (83%)
```

## Difficulty Classification

### Easy
- Clear, unambiguous stage expressions
- Single emotion straightforwardly stated
- Typical language markers strongly present
- Lowest cognitive demand for classification

### Medium  
- Some stage ambiguity (possible adjacent stage)
- Multiple emotions or complex emotional blends
- Less typical phrasing
- Moderate classification challenge

### Hard
- Stage boundary or transition scenarios
- Complex spiritual/existential language
- Unusual cultural or tradition-specific framing
- Highest demand for accurate interpretation
